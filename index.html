1992, the Adoption Law of China was promulgated. It specified the quality that must be satisfied for the establishment of an adoptive relationship. It required the adopter should be childless, under 30, capable of rearing and educating the adoptee, and no illness. Furthermore, in Article 21, it enabled the right for foreigners to adopt a child from China. Since that, international adoption became legal in China. In the next three years from 1992 to 1995, the total number of intercountry adoption in China rose rapidly from 252 to 3,000.

The requirement for foreign adopters was slightly different from Chinese adopters. The difference lay in the demand of childless. Before the newest amendment for Measures on the Registration of Adoption of Children by Foreigners in China, healthy foreign couples who were above 35, with an appropriate reason of adoption and stable economic status, would usually be accepted after they delivered all documents.

The US is the dominant country adopting children from China. In fact, the US has been the state who received the most international adoptees all the time from 1990. The Top 4 countries for receiving intercountry adoption has long been: USA, Italy, France, Canada with figure of 336,296, 56,836, 75,379, 39,273 respectively from 1990 to 2013.

International adoption first got its initial scale after World War II, since many children turned orphans during the war. In 1995, as a couple, Bertha and Henry Holt adopted eight children from the Korean War. This produced controversy in America society and other families followed the action. It gradually developed into a tradition.

The Holts believed they were doing God’s work, but they became lightning rods for controversy about how adoptive families should be made. In the press, the Holts were portrayed as heroic, selfless figures. In Congress, Oregon Senator Richard Neuberger called them incarnations of “the Biblical Good Samaritan.”

Like cited above ‘Holts believed they were doing God’s work’, a lot of people in the US believed in God. They have the faith to spread the kindness and save the orphans offering them a happy family.

In that period, the population and the birth rate of the US has in decline already. So in terms of population, it is tolerable to adopt abundant children. And United Sates is a society full of diversity and immigration, it has the atmosphere to embrace people from other culture.

The fee to adopt a child from China is comparatively affordable. The total fee is around 30, 000 dollars, including application fee, document fee, investigation of family background fee, fingerprint recording and all the politic procedures in China, etc. Among these, 5,000 dollars are for the orphanages. While the fee for other countries can up to 40,000 or 50,000.

Then with the progress of globalization, the human rights of China have been a concern for many countries. In 1995, Channel Four broadcasted a controversial documentary The Dying Rooms in the UK. It drew the sufferings of abandoned children who live in welfare institutions with poor environment and facilities in China. It arose the attention for Chinese orphans and boost the trend to adopt Chinese children.

The data from the US Bureau of Consular Affairs showed that in 1999, Russia was the nation sending the most adoptees at 4,381. But it increased quickly to surpass Russia in the next year. In 2005, the adoptees from China reached with the figure of 7,903. However, since 2006, it decreased year by year. In 2015, the number is down to 2354.

This change resulted in the amendment for adoption qualities for foreigners in 2005. After this amendment, the people or the couple must be above 35 while under 50 and must be married. Other requirements include no more than two divorces are allowed, and body Mass should not beyond index of 40, etc. All the amendment has accumulated its complexity to adopt a Chinese orphans now.
